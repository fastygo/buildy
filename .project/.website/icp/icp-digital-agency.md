# ICP: Digital Agency / Delivery Studio

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Company type | Web agency, product studio, FastyGo implementation partner |
| Team size | 3–30 delivery staff |
| Client mix | SaaS startups, local businesses upgrading to CMS-backed sites |
| Budget | Per-project or studio subscription; margin on delivery speed |
| Technical maturity | Mixed; senior devs hand off CMS/admin to juniors |

## Company profile

A studio that sells **client projects**, not just design. They need repeatable delivery: discovery → prototype → client approval → implementation → handoff. They are sensitive to **margin per project** and **reuse of patterns** across clients.

Generic AI builders help pitch fast but hurt handoff: client gets unmaintainable JS, no admin, agency cannot standardize support.

## Jobs to be done

1. Win pitches with **working previews** during sales cycle.
2. Standardize on **FastyGo + AppCMS** deliverables for supportability.
3. Export **client-owned GitHub repos** with documented content model.
4. Train client marketing on **AppCMS admin** for post-launch edits.

## Why BuildY wins

- **Template and pattern reuse** across client projects (landing blocks, content types).
- Clear **file tree and diffs** for agency QA before delivery.
- CMS-backed sites reduce "call us to change a headline" support tickets.
- Positions agency as modern: AI-assisted speed + professional stack.

## Why BuildY loses

- Agency sells WordPress/Webflow-only and refuses Go hosting story.
- Clients demand exclusive React/Next deliverables.
- No in-house capacity to support AppCMS runtime.

## Typical buying committee

| Role | Involvement |
|------|-------------|
| Agency principal / delivery lead | Buyer |
| Lead developer | Stack evaluator |
| Account / project manager | End user on client projects |

## Success metrics

- Proposal-to-preview time cut by 50%+  
- ≥2 reusable templates per vertical (e.g. SaaS landing, services site)  
- Client handoff checklist completed (repo, CMS admin, docs)  
- Support hours reduced for content-only changes  

## Market framing

**Client delivery** positioning: landing velocity plus **agency-grade export and CMS admin** as the deliverable boundary.
