# Buyer Persona: Founder / CEO

## Profile

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Title | Founder, co-founder, CEO (often acting PM) |
| Company | Pre-seed / seed SaaS, 1–8 FTE |
| Reports to | Board, investors, themselves |
| Tools today | Notion, Figma, ChatGPT, maybe Webflow or AI builder trial |
| BuildY role | Economic buyer; often also primary end user |

## Goals

- Demonstrate traction with a **credible product URL** before next fundraise or launch.
- Move fast without hiring a full eng team prematurely.
- Retain **optionality**: first hire should extend, not replace, what's built.
- Control narrative: pricing, features, social proof updatable without dev bottleneck.

## Pain points

- AI builders produce **pretty but brittle** sites; every copy change feels like a dev task.
- Agencies are slow and expensive for MVP landings.
- Technical cofounder gap creates fear of wrong stack choice.
- Investors ask "where's the admin / where's the product" — a static page isn't enough.

## BuildY value proposition

> "Ship a CMS-backed SaaS landing and app skeleton in days. Own the Go/Templ source on GitHub. Your first engineer continues in IDE — nothing thrown away."

## Decision criteria

1. Speed to first preview  
2. Professional visual quality (Templ + Tailwind patterns)  
3. Export and repo ownership  
4. CMS for marketing self-serve  
5. Price vs agency / additional headcount  

## Triggers to buy

- Launch deadline or fundraise dataroom  
- Failed attempt with generic AI builder (hardcoded content)  
- First engineering hire starting in 4–8 weeks — needs clean handoff  

## Messaging contrast (category vs BuildY)

| Common AI-builder angle | BuildY angle |
|-------------------------|--------------|
| "AI cofounder" | "Prototype workspace + real FastyGo stack" |
| "One-click publish" | "Export → deploy AppCMS app on your infra" |
| "No technical cofounder needed" | "No eng team needed for **landing + model**; eng for **product depth**" |

## Quotes we want to earn

- "I showed a live landing with editable blocks, not a Figma link."
- "Our hire cloned the repo and shipped auth in week one — the landing wasn't throwaway."
